My chosen axes for this project separate out companies based on 2 factors. The X-axis separates companies that tend to be resilient to economic pressure from those that tend to depend a lot more on the state of the overall economy. The Y-axis separates companies that primarily work in the digital space from those with a focus on digital products. These may seem closely related at first glance, but it actually varies a lot based on industry. 
Unsurprisingly, Information Technology companies took up the majority of the top-right of the graph, as they tend to be seen as more risky investments and are fully digital, and consumer discretionary companies tend to be more resilient. Surprisingly, however, many Industrial companies were in the volatile-physical quadrant of the graph. This is surprising because the industrial supply chain tends to be seen as more reliable, as its raw materials costs and consumer base tend not to move as much as other economic factors. 
A third axis could be centralization, looking at companies that are more tightly vertically integrated, separated from companies that tend to be part of wider ecosystems outside of their control. An example from this dataset would be MGM Resorts, which owns and controls the majority of its assets, against a company like Microsoft, which relies of components from across the world to build their products
